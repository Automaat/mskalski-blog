Hi, my name is Marcin Skalski. I am an infrastructure engineer from Cracow, Poland, working on Kuma Service Mesh and Kong Mesh at Kong. Most of my work sits somewhere between distributed systems, Kubernetes, observability, and the practical mess of running platforms in production.

I am a core maintainer of CNCF Kuma. Over the last few years I have designed service mesh features, observability systems, performance tooling, docs, and APIs that people actually have to debug at 2 a.m. Before Kong, I built and led the service mesh platform team at Allegro. That platform supported more than 1,500 services and roughly 2 million requests per second, so it left very little room for hand-wavy engineering.

Lately, I keep coming back to agentic AI and LLM systems. Not the polished demo version. The part that interests me is the harness around the model: tools, memory, evals, traces, feedback loops, human review, and all the failure modes that show up once an agent touches a real codebase. At Kong, I help engineers use AI coding tools in daily work, especially Claude Code.

Outside work, I build my own agent experiments. Some are useful, some are probably overbuilt, and some exist because I wanted to see where they would break. They include LangGraph agents, MCP servers, local LLM tooling, and assistants that can use real tools instead of only chatting.

I also speak and write about engineering. I have spoken at KubeCon EU, GeeCON, 4Developers, DevDays Europe, Allegro Tech Meeting, and smaller community events. I host a Polish YouTube channel, "Głęboki Odczyt", where I read and discuss computer science papers about LLMs, distributed systems, and networking.

When I am not around code, I am usually taking photos, learning, traveling, or following some technical question further than I planned. I still like understanding how things work. The subject keeps changing.
